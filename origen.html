ORIGEN OF GAME

My youngest picked her favorite restaurant for lunch one day. There was a long wait for her bento box. During that long wait the dreaded wait for food appeared and so did the most dreaded game in our household.

It’s really a great game called “Guess the Animal”. It teaches kids about animals, not just want they say or what color they are but about any kind of related information like what a mammal is and anything else you can think of as you expand their knowledge and curiosity futrher and further.

Both kids loved it. I did too but as a grown-up it is hard to play that game nearly every day and at every opportunity,

So while we waited with only white, blue, pink and yellow packets of sugar and sweetener for amusement for my four year old, a new game was invented. Different names were assigned to the sugar packets and different relationships to this colorful packets.

This game was called “Carbon has four friends”.

Carbon was white and had four arms. He wants to hold hands with four friends.

Hydrogen was blue had just one arm and cold only hold one hand.

To play, some number of white carbons were put on the table in a row. Then the child would figure out how many blue hydrogens to add.

After playing back and forth as it is very much fun for the child to make the grownup do the same things, then a new atom was added, oxygen.

After doing simple chains, we worked on pardon played with simple branched chains. Sometimes I would cover my eyes while my daughter would remove some for me to guess which ones were missing.

That is the inspiration for this page and a start for the kid’s chemistry page I hope to do one day. I hope you enjoy.

Sciences is not supposed to be hard. It is supposed to be fun.
